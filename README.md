## Why I chose Vocallabs over Subspace

Both companies are genuinely interesting, but Vocallabs was the sharper choice for this assignment for three reasons:

**1. The product gap is more visible from the outside.**  
Subspace.money is a consumer fintech app — the friction points are mostly inside the app (subscription management flows, group sharing UX). Vocallabs is a B2B SaaS product whose *entire* go-to-market surface is public: website, pricing page, blog, GitHub, Play Store listing, docs. That means a rigorous teardown is possible without needing an active paid account. Every observation is verifiable by anyone reading this report.

**2. The moat vs. execution gap is more interesting to analyse.**  
Subspace's moat (network effects, bootstrapped profitability, ₹36.5Cr ARR) is largely already working — the product teardown would mostly be UX polish suggestions. Vocallabs has a *claimed* moat (India-first language tuning, full-stack analytics, data flywheel) that is not yet visible in their GTM or product surface. That gap — between what a product *is* and what it *shows* — is the more challenging and more valuable product management problem to dissect.

**3. The competitive landscape is richer and more actionable.**  
The India voice AI market in 2026 has a clearly legible competitor map: VAPI and Retell (global, USD-priced, no India compliance), Bolna and Caller Digital (India-tuned, developer-first), Gnani (enterprise BFSI). Vocallabs is fighting a real, named battle with specific positioning gaps. Analysing Subspace's competitive landscape would require mapping a more fragmented consumer fintech space with less obvious strategic choices.

**Bottom line:** Vocallabs gave me a richer, more specific set of observations — the kind the assignment brief explicitly rewards over generic feedback.

## Methodology

- Walked through vocallabs.ai homepage, blog, pricing page, docs, and contact flow
- Reviewed the Vocallabs Android app (Play Store listing)
- Explored the Vocallabs GitHub org (`github.com/Vocallabsai`) — 4 public repos including VocalFlow (open-source call flow builder), vocal-sdk, n8n-nodes-vocallabs, and a Chrome extension
- Benchmarked against VAPI, Retell AI, Bolna, Caller Digital, Gnani, and Sarvam on: pricing model, India language depth, compliance posture, and onboarding flow
- Applied Porter's Five Forces to assess competitive intensity in India voice AI
- Prioritised feedbacks by Impact vs. Effort
